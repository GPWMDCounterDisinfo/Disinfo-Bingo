Common Disinformation Tactics

  • Fabrication & Manipulation:
    • Deepfakes/AI: Generating realistic fake audio, video, or images.
    • Cheapfakes: Editing real media (e.g., slowing down a video) to alter meaning.
    • Contextual Manipulation: Presenting real facts or images out of context to mislead (e.g., using old photos as current).

  • Amplification & Obfuscation:
    • Flooding: Overwhelming the information space with contradictory stories to cause confusion.
    • Bots & Trolls: Using automated accounts or coordinated networks to boost false content.
    • Astroturfing: Creating fake grassroots movements to lend false credibility.

  • Exploiting Psychology & Bias:
    • Confirmation Bias: Crafting messages to appeal to pre-existing beliefs.
    • Emotional Appeals: Using fear, anger, or outrage to bypass critical thinking.
    • Filter Bubbles: Targeting specific groups within echo chambers.

  • Discrediting & Silencing:
    • Smearing: Attacking reliable sources (journalists, scientists) or individuals.
    • Impersonation: Faking legitimate sources or expert personas.

  • Strategic Deception:
    • Cherry-Picking Data: Using selective data to support a false narrative (common in climate denial).
    • Planting False Narratives: Spreading outright lies or forged documents (like Operation Mincemeat).
